Facts

Most of the plastic that ends up in the ocean comes from river in low-to-middle income countries.

The majority of ocean-bound plastic pollution originates from rivers flowing through low-to-middle income countries, highlighting the global challenge of addressing plastic waste at its source. Efforts to combat this issue must prioritize international collaboration and support for these regions in managing their plastic waste effectively.

2 million plastic bags are used every minute worldwide.

This widespread consumption shows the urgent need for sustainable alternatives and greater awareness to reduce the environmental impact of single-use plastics.

Every minute of every day a truckload of plastic is dumped into the ocean.

This constant influx of plastic pollution not only devastates marine ecosystems, but also poses a serious threat to the health of our planet and future generations. Urgent global action is needed to address this alarming issue and protect the world's oceans from further harm.
